NASW Values

Value: Dignity and Worth of the Person

Understanding that just because someone is incarcerated does not mean that she should be treated less than a human especially when she is pregnant or recently delivered. The women’s body during these times are the most vulnerable and need the most care. By giving these women the care and time with their children even while serving their sentence. It helps to continue to strengthen the parents-child relationship.

Value: Importance of Human Relationships

The most valuable relationship a person can have or will have is with their mother. A relationship that starts far beyond the time of birth. A child’s relationship with their mother starts in the womb. When a mother breathes she's breathing for two. When she eat she's eating for two. So having a mother incarcerated already hurts the relationship. Thankfully they understood the importance of allowing the mothers and children to still have that time. And ensure that even in ail that their mother's safety and best interest are handled. In order to help both the mother and child feel comfortable in the situation they are in.
